April 25th is World Malaria Day - a day when the world recognizes efforts to control the spread of malaria. The most recent statistics for worldwide rates of malaria (from 2105) show a 21% decrease in the number of new cases of malaria and a 29% reduction in the number of deaths due to malaria.

While this is good news, researchers are concerned about the spread of drug resistance among the Plasmodium organsim that causes malaria. A spread of drug resistance can result in an increase in the number of cases of malaria.
