The microsporidians are another recent addition to the Kingdom Fungi.  They are all intracellular parasites of animals or protists, mainly affecting invertebrates. They do not have mitochondria, but they do have mitosomes.  The presence of mitosomes, along with other fungal characteristics, distinguishes this group.  What group do you think they were previously classified in (hint: think back to the Protists)? Microsporidians have no means of locomotion, and are able to form spores that can live outside of the host cell for long periods of time.  Human microsporidiosis usually affects only people with compromised immune systems.  There is research under way to see if a mosquito-specific microsporidian could be used as a way to control malaria.
