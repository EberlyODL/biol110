Introduction and Learning Objectives

Mendel was able interpret the results of his genetic crosses because he took careful notes, made accurate counts, and chose traits that exhibited a clear dominant or recessive expression pattern. In fact, alleles that show simple dominant or recessive expression patterns are sometimes called "Mendelian traits".  Many alleles, however, do not behave in such a simple Mendelian manner. This tutorial will explore more complex patterns of expression as well as the application of genetic principles to the development of pedigrees. By the end of this tutorial you should have a basic understanding of:

  • Genes with multiple alleles
  • The distinctions between the three types of dominance
  • How genes can have multiple effects
  • How genes can affect the expression of other genes
  • How some characters are controlled by more than one gene
  • Pedigree analysis
  • Cystic fibrosis
  • Huntington's disease
  • Sickle-cell disease
  • Phenylketonuria

Performance Objectives

  • Compare and contrast the different dominance relationships that alleles can show
  • Discuss the phenomenon of pleiotropy and its effect on an organism’s phenotype
  • Explain how epistasis affects the expression of genes
  • Describe polygenic inheritance
  • Identify the symbols used in a pedigree analysis
  • Identify different patterns of inheritance seen in humans using specific genes (cystic fibrosis, PKU, sickle cell anemia, and Huntington’s)
