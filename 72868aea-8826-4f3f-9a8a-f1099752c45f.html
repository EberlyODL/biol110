Prokaryotes II Part 2 VoiceThread Transcript

Slide 1
While prokaryotes are structurally more simple than eukaryotes - metabolically they are more diverse and capable of a much wider array of metabolic modes.

There are four basic metabolic modes - photoautotrophy, chemoautotrophy, photoheterotrophy, and chemoheterotrophy.

Slide 2
Eukaryotic organisms are only capable of two of these metabolic modes - photoautrophy as we see in the form of photosynthesis in the plants and chemoheterotrophy as we see in animals.

Slide 3
Metabolic modes are based on where an organism gets its energy and where it gets its carbon.  Energy can either come from sunlight or energy-rich molecules.  Carbon can either come from carbon dioxide or organic molecules.

Plants use sunlight for energy and carbon dioxide for carbon and are classified as photoautotrophs.  Animals use organic molecules as both a carbon source and an energy source and are classified as chemoheterotrophs.  

Bacteria can use photoautotrophy and chemoheterotrophy but they can also be photoheterotrophs and chemoautotrophs.

Slide 4
These diverse metabolic modes allow prokaryotes to live in diverse environments that in general do not support eukaryotic life such as hot springs, hydrothermal vents, and salt ponds.  There are even bacteria that can survive inside nuclear reactors.
