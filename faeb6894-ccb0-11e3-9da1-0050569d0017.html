One final consideration when interpreting a graph(s) is to make sure that the scales of the axes are appropriate--especially when comparing multiple data sets. This is a form of trickery that is often used in the media and in advertising, where the scales are changed to enhance or reduce an effect.

Take the North Atlantic storm data once again and see what happens when the axis changes.

It becomes immediately apparent that the trend looks far less dramatic when a scale of 0-100 is used as opposed to 7-15. If the Y-axis units were conveniently omitted (which happens a lot when people are trying to push a point of view), you would never know that the graph on the left was hiding the true data trend.
