Will sub-sampling not provide a biased estimate of the population?

To answer this you need to step back and look at populations in nature. In nature, most populations have what is called a normal distribution. Put simply, it turns out that middle values tend to occur more frequently, with outliers, or extreme values, occurring less frequently. This is why you don't see basketball player-sized people as frequently as you see average height individuals. Let's illustrate what we mean by looking at the distribution of IQ scores in a population.

So, in theory, if you take enough individuals in your sample, you should get more of the middle values, and therefore, a result that resembles that of the population. The million-dollar question, however, is how many is enough for the sample to be representative of the population?

Now that you are more familiar with the difference between a sample and a population, you can start to address this question.

Returning to the 'Party School' example. If there are an estimated 14 million students in US universities, 122,000 represents just less than 1% of the US university population. Moreover, on average, there were 325 respondents to the survey per school, which in Penn State's case amounts to less than 1% of the University Park campus' enrollment of 43,000 students.

Based on this information, would you consider the sample size to be sufficient to draw a definitive conclusion?

While you may like to say yes (due to the pride of going to the top party school), I am sure you will agree that a sample of less than 1% of the population may not be adequate in this case.

So if you are going to enjoy wearing this new party badge, keep in mind the actual validity behind the results.

It is therefore important to keep in mind that the primary point of sampling is to create a small group from a population that is as similar to the larger population as possible. In essence, you want to have a little group that is like the big group. With that in mind, one of the features you look for in a sample is the degree of representativeness--how well does the sample represent the larger population from which it was drawn?
