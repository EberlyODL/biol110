Now that you have a better idea of how data is gathered, lets look at how is it treated once gathered.

First, let's introduce the common different types of data that you may have, or will come across.

  • Binary data - generally Yes/No.
  • Categorical data - where individuals are placed into categories e.g. male and female.
  • Continuous data - most commonly used where finer measurements are taken e.g. weight, blood pressure, speed etc.

Binary and categorical data are commonly used in social science and behavioral science, whereas continuous data is commonly used in the life sciences.

Examples:

  • Binary - questionnaire - have you ever suffered from a heart condition? (Yes/No); did you graduate from high school? (Yes/No).
  • Categorical - smoking status (none, light, heavy); hair color (blond, brown, black, red).
  • Continuous - weight gain, size of fish, death rates, carbon emissions per country.
